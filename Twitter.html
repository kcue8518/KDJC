Twitter Hacks

Background

Hackers gained access to the accounts of significant public figures and organizations, including Joe Biden, Barack Obama, Elon Musk, Bill Gates, Jeff Bezos, and Apple, thanks to a huge security breach on Twitter. The corporation blamed it on a "planned social engineering operation" on its own staff, which allowed the hackers to get access to "internal systems and tools." The hack lasted several hours, and in order to put an end to it, Twitter disabled all verified accounts from tweeting at all, an unprecedented move. Most accounts had been restored, but the business cautioned that it "may take further actions." The hijacked accounts, which have tens of millions of followers, published a series of tweets outlining a typical bitcoin con: Followers were told that if they contributed cryptocurrency to a certain bitcoin wallet, they would get twice the money back. Twitter has become the world's de facto wire service, with governments using it for official communications in times of crisis; a breach on the scale of Wednesday's incident
